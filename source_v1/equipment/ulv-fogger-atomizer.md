# ULV Fogger Atomizer

## Purpose
The Ultra Low Volume (ULV) fogger atomizer applies liquid antimicrobial, disinfectant, and odor control products as a fine mist (typically 5–50 micron droplets). It is used to treat surfaces and air spaces that are difficult to reach with conventional spraying, especially after water damage or during mold remediation.

ULV fogging is a **supplemental treatment tool**, not a replacement for proper extraction, cleaning, and drying.

## When to Use
- After gross water removal on Category 2 or Category 3 losses.
- During mold remediation on non-porous and semi-porous surfaces inside containment.
- For odor neutralization in affected areas.
- Treating wall cavities, under cabinets, crawl spaces, or other confined/hard-to-reach areas (via direct application or injection ports).
- Applying EPA-registered antimicrobials or odor counteractants when label instructions allow fogging.

## When NOT to Use
- As a shortcut instead of proper demo, extraction, and drying.
- On surfaces that will be disturbed or removed before the required dwell/contact time.
- Without following the specific product’s EPA label and SDS exactly.
- In occupied spaces or without proper re-entry protocols.
- Near sensitive electronics, artwork, or materials that cannot tolerate added moisture or residue.

## Key Safety Requirements
- Full PPE is mandatory: appropriate respirator (minimum N95, often P100 or higher depending on the chemical), gloves, eye protection, and Tyvek or equivalent suit.
- Evacuate all people and pets from the treatment area.
- Post clear “Do Not Enter” signage with the product-specific re-entry time.
- Turn off and isolate the HVAC system. Seal returns and supplies as needed.
- Never fog near open flames, sparks, or energized electrical equipment.
- Account for the added moisture the fog introduces — especially on sensitive materials or when drying timelines are tight.

## Operation Guidelines
1. Calculate the cubic volume of the space and the amount of diluted product required.
2. Mix the product exactly according to the label instructions. Never guess on dilution.
3. Start at the point farthest from the exit and work systematically toward the door.
4. Keep the fogger moving in slow, slightly overlapping passes. Avoid heavy buildup in one spot.
5. Maintain the correct droplet size for the goal (smaller droplets for air treatment, slightly larger for surface contact).
6. Allow the full labeled dwell/contact time before re-entry or continuing work.
7. After fogging, ventilate or run air movers/exhaust fans as needed to help surfaces dry and reduce residue.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Using the wrong product or incorrect dilution ratio.
- Over-fogging and leaving heavy residue that later causes issues.
- Entering the space before the required re-entry time.
- Treating fogging as a substitute for actual cleaning and drying.
- Not accounting for the extra moisture added to the environment.
- Fogging without proper containment when cross-contamination is a concern.

## Maintenance
- Empty and thoroughly rinse the tank after every use.
- Flush the lines and nozzle with clean water (or per manufacturer recommendation).
- Clean or replace filters regularly.
- Store the unit with a light protective oil coating on metal parts if recommended by the manufacturer to prevent corrosion.
- Check batteries, cords, and seals before each job.

## Notes
- Always defer to the specific product label. If the label does not list fogging as an approved application method, do not use the ULV fogger with that product.
- Document the product name, dilution, amount used, application time, and re-entry time in your job documentation.
- Real-world observation: Many techs under-estimate how much residue a heavy fog leaves behind. When in doubt, use less product and make a second lighter pass if needed.
