# equipment/air-filtration-devices.md — Air Filtration Devices (Practical Field Guide)

**Purpose**: Understand when and how to use air filtration devices (air scrubbers / negative air machines) on restoration jobs, especially mold and Category 3 water losses.

These units help control airborne contaminants and can create negative pressure to contain affected areas.

## What Air Filtration Devices Do

**Primary functions**:

1. **HEPA Filtration**: Capture fine particles, mold spores, dust, and some smoke particles from the air.
2. **Negative Air / Containment**: Exhaust filtered air outside (or to a safe location) to pull air into the contained area from cleaner parts of the building. This helps prevent contaminated air from migrating to unaffected areas.
3. **Air Cleaning**: Reduce airborne contaminants inside the work area.

**My working rule**: On mold jobs and Category 3 water losses, I consider air filtration early, especially when doing demo or when the affected area is connected to the rest of the house.

## Types of Units

**Air Scrubbers**:
- Designed to clean the air inside a space.
- Usually recirculate filtered air back into the room.
- Good for reducing airborne particles during and after work.

**Negative Air Machines**:
- Designed to exhaust air out of the contained area.
- Create negative pressure when properly set up with containment.
- Often used with ducting to exhaust outside or to a safe/unoccupied area.

**My working rule**: On containment jobs, I prefer units that can be used in negative air mode. On jobs where I mainly want to clean the air inside the space, a good air scrubber works well.

## When to Use Air Filtration

**Strongly recommended**:
- Visible mold or suspected hidden mold
- Category 3 (black water) losses
- Heavy demo in contaminated areas
- Jobs where you want to limit migration of spores/odors/particles to unaffected areas

**Often helpful**:
- Large or complex water losses with high airborne dust
- Smoke odor jobs (can help reduce some particles)
- Occupied homes where you want to improve air quality in the work area

**My working rule**: On any job with visible mold or Category 3 water, I plan on using air filtration during demo and while the area is disturbed.

## Setup and Placement

**For negative air / containment**:
- Place the unit inside the contained area.
- Exhaust filtered air outside the building (preferred) or to a safe/unoccupied area.
- Use containment (plastic, zip walls, etc.) to create a controlled environment.
- Monitor pressure differential if possible (you want the contained area to be under negative pressure).

**For general air cleaning**:
- Place the unit where it can process air from the work area effectively.
- Ensure good airflow to the intake.
- Change or clean filters according to manufacturer recommendations and job conditions.

**My working rule**: On containment jobs, I set up negative air before doing heavy demo whenever possible. It’s much easier to contain contamination than to clean it up after it spreads.

## Filter Considerations

Most quality units use multi-stage filtration:
- Pre-filter (captures larger particles)
- Secondary filter
- HEPA filter (captures very fine particles including many mold spores)

**My working rule**: I check filters regularly on active jobs. Dirty or overloaded filters reduce effectiveness and can even push contaminants through if they’re damaged.

## Common Mistakes

- Using air filtration as a substitute for proper containment and source removal.
- Poor exhaust setup (exhausting contaminated air into clean areas).
- Not changing filters often enough on dirty jobs.
- Running units without proper containment (limited benefit).
- Ignoring the need for negative pressure on mold and Cat 3 jobs.

**My working rule**: Air filtration is a tool to support containment and cleaning — not a replacement for good containment practices and removing contaminated materials.

## Safety Notes

- Follow manufacturer guidelines for filter changes and maintenance.
- Be careful when changing heavily loaded filters (they can release particles).
- Make sure exhaust is directed to a safe location.
- Use proper PPE when working in contaminated environments.
