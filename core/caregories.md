# core/categories.md — Categories of Water (My Working Definitions)

**Purpose**: Quick field identification of water category to drive PPE, containment, and overall approach. Written in plain language for voice use.

## Category 1 — Clean Water

**What it usually is**:
- Busted supply lines (toilet, fridge ice maker, sink supply)
- Rainwater that hasn't contacted the ground or contaminants
- Some AC condensate or clean appliance leaks (if truly clean)

**Field read**:
- Clear or only slightly cloudy
- No strong odor
- Source is obviously clean plumbing or rainwater

**My approach**:
- Standard PPE (gloves, eye protection, possibly light respirator if dusty)
- Normal containment unless other factors present
- Still extract aggressively if there's volume — clean water can become dirty fast once it sits and picks up dirt from the structure

**Voice note**: "If it's coming straight from a supply line and looks clean, I'm calling it Category 1 unless it has been sitting long enough to grow stuff."

## Category 2 — Gray Water

**What it usually is**:
- Water from washing machines, dishwashers, showers, sinks, or other "used" but not sewage sources
- Can contain soaps, food particles, urine, or mild contaminants

**Field read**:
- Often cloudy or has visible particles
- May have a soapy or musty smell
- Source is an appliance or drain line

**My approach**:
- Higher PPE (respirator recommended, especially if it's been sitting)
- Containment setup before heavy traffic or demo
- Treat as higher risk for bacterial growth if it sits more than 24-48 hours
- Still usually salvageable with good extraction and drying, but don't take it lightly

**Voice note**: "Gray water jobs get containment and better PPE. The line between Cat 2 and Cat 3 blurs fast if it's been wet a while."

## Category 3 — Black Water

**What it usually is**:
- Sewage backups
- Flood water that has contacted ground, feces, or decaying organic matter
- Long-standing water with visible mold, slime, or strong odor
- Any water where you can't confidently rule out heavy contamination

**Field read**:
- Obvious sewage smell or appearance
- Visible mold growth already present
- Source is toilet overflow with solids, sewer line, or exterior flood with debris
- Long-term hidden leak that has had time to grow biology

**My approach** (non-negotiable on these):
- Full PPE: respirator (N95 minimum, P100 or better if heavy contamination), tyvek or equivalent suit, gloves, boot covers
- Proper containment / negative air if the affected area allows
- Often more aggressive material removal rather than trying to dry everything in place
- Document heavily. These jobs have higher liability and health risk

**Voice note**: "If there's any doubt and it smells like shit or has been sitting with visible growth, I'm treating it as Category 3 until proven otherwise. PPE and containment first."

## Quick Field Rule (My Standard)

When in doubt → err toward the **higher** category for PPE and containment decisions. You can always relax later with more information. You cannot easily go back and add protection after you've tracked contamination through the rest of the house.

**Timeline effect**: Any water that sits long enough (especially >48-72 hours in warm conditions) can move up in effective category due to bacterial and mold growth. A fresh Category 1 can become a de facto Category 3 problem if ignored.
